Hawaii has seen a steady rise in population over the past couple decades. It is fair to assume that its population will continue to rise. Sharp increases in population growth can be found directly after a economic or cultural shock.

The graph below demonstrates this phenomenon clearly following 2000 and the 2008 recession. Population is numbered by the thousands of people.

The number of hosts on AirBnb similarly has grown greatly over time. The biggest change can be seen at the year 2010. Where both the number of hosts and the price of rentals grew expoenetially.

Here we can see the number of residential building permits that were distributed each year starting from 1975. The general trend over time has shown that less and less building permits are being approved. This may be attributed to the short supply of land on the islands.
